Major Events in Bowie's Life
  • 1947: David Hayward-Jones is born on January 8 in Brixton, London.
  • 1959: David recieves his first instrument for Christmas, a plastic saxophone.
  • 1962: During a fight with a friend, David is punched in the eye, causing his iconic pupil to remain undialated.
  • 1966: David chooses the stage name of David Bowie.
  • 1967: Bowie debuts with a self-titled album.
  • 1969: Space Oddity is released. The titular track famously echoed the words "Ground control to Major Tom" in the annals of history.
  • 1972: Bowie releases Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders From Mars, perhaps his most recognizable album.
  • 1974: Diamond Dogs is released 10 years before its setting.
  • 1977: "Heroes" is released.
  • 1981: Bowie and Queen collaborate to create "Under Pressure".
  • 1986: Bowie stars in Jim Henson's "Labyrinth".
  • 1992: Bowie marries model Imam, whom he remains with until his death.
  • 1996: Bowie is inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • 2004: In Germany, Bowie suffers a heart attack on stage.
  • 2016: Bowie releases Blackstar on January 8, his birthday, which is his 25th and final studio album.
  • 2016: On January 10, two days after his birthday and final album release, Bowie dies from a cancer-related complication.
